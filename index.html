FAQ

  • What is the ACMPR?
    What is the ACMPR?

    The Access to Cannibis for Medical Purposes Regulations (ACMPR) is name given by Health Canada to the current federal medical marijuana program. The program was adopted from the MMPR (2013-Aug 2016) in August 2016.The program is a set of regulations designed to give qualified patients access to safe, high quality medical cannabis.

  • Is Medical Cannabis Safe?
    Is Medical Cannabis Safe?

    Studies have shown a wide variety of recognized therapeutic uses for medical cannabis. We recommend you consult with your Health Care Practitioner to learn if it is right for you.

  • How do I use Medical Cannabis?
    HOW DO I USE MEDICAL CANNABIS?

    There are three common ways to use medical cannabis: smoking, vaporizing and oral consumption. Cannabis oil is commonly applied directly under the tongue or incorporated in recipes as to be edible.

    Smoking, the most common way of consumption, is not recommended as research suggests it significant increases your risk of cancer and respiratory disease. (Canadian Cancer Society)

    A vaporizer functions by heating the cannabis to boiling temperature. The cannabis will vaporize the active ingredients of the plant material, which, can then be inhaled. A vaporizer dehydrates the cannabis and causes it to release its yield without catching firing and subsequently reducing smoke inhalation.

    Oral consumption has gained significant popularity in recent years. Cannabis oil is processed differently by the human body and tends to provide users with sensations throughout the entire body. Individuals report the effects of cannabis oil last longer than when cannabis is smoked or vaporized.
